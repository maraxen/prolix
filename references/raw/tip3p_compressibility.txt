Sprint 6 NPT Research: TIP3P Isothermal Compressibility at 300K, 1 atm
Fetched: 2026-04-26

== GROMACS canonical value ==
Source: https://manual.gromacs.org/current/user-guide/mdp-options.html

Direct quote: "The compressibility (NOTE: this is now really in bar⁻¹)
For water at 1 atm and 300 K the compressibility is 4.5e-5 bar⁻¹."

This is used universally in GROMACS tutorials and production TIP3P simulations.
Value for prolix: WATER_COMPRESSIBILITY_300K_BAR_INV = 4.5e-5 (safe, standard)

== TIP3P-specific vs experimental β ==
Experimental water at 300K, 1 atm: β ≈ 4.57e-5 bar⁻¹ (NIST)
TIP3P from MD simulations: β ≈ 6.2–7.6e-5 bar⁻¹

Sources (not directly retrieved — publisher access denied):
  Vega & Abascal 2011, Phys. Chem. Chem. Phys. 13, 19663
  Mark & Nilsson 2001, J. Phys. Chem. A 105, 9954

TIP3P is known to be ~35–70% more compressible than real water — a systematic error
of the TIP3P force field model.

== Practical guidance ==
Using 4.5e-5 bar⁻¹ for TIP3P NPT barostat:
  - SAFE: barostat is robust to this degree of compressibility mismatch
  - STANDARD: universally used in GROMACS TIP3P simulations
  - Minor effect: affects pressure-fluctuation amplitude, not density or stability

Using TIP3P-specific ~6.2e-5 bar⁻¹:
  - More accurate NPT volume fluctuations
  - Not required for stability or density accuracy
  - Use for high-accuracy NPT pressure-fluctuation studies

== Pressure dependence over 0–200 bar ==
Sub-1% variation expected — single constant is appropriate for Sprint 6.
