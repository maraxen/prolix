### 1. LFMiddle Hypothesis (Phase 3) — **Recommended first**

**Goal**: Test whether the Leimkuhler-Matthews O-step splitting reduces SETTLE+thermostat coupling enough to lift the dt ≤ 0.5 fs constraint.

**Why now**: The dt cap is the most user-visible limitation. The hypothesis is falsifiable in a single sweep — implement LFMiddle, run dt ∈ {0.25, 0.5, 1.0} fs, check temperature stability.

**Deliverables**:
- Add force-step marker in `step_registry` (enable mid-step force recompute)
- Register `lfmiddle_langevin` sequence in `make_integrator`
- dt-sweep test: 0.25, 0.5, 1.0 fs; pass criterion ≈ ±5 K stability over 50 ps

**Estimated effort**: 2–3 days  
**Risk**: Exploratory — may not resolve the coupling. Outcome is still informative either way.

### 2. NPT KE Initialization Bug (Phase 6 precursor)

**Goal**: Diagnose why T ≈ 5000 K at the very first NPT record (0.1 ps), well before any long-run drift accumulates.

**New finding (cluster run 13967161, 2026-05-15)**: The previously documented "diverges after ~10 ps" description understates the problem. Temperature is already 16× target at the first recorded step. This points to KE inflation at the NVT→NPT handoff (box rescaling / momentum scaling on initialization), not gradual accumulation.

**Why this matters**: If the root cause is a bad initialization (e.g., momentum not rescaled after box resize, or CSVR pulling from a stale KE estimate), it may be a small targeted fix rather than the full CSVR redesign anticipated in Phase 6.

**Suggested first step**: Add diagnostic logging of per-atom KE immediately before and after the first NPT step to localize the inflation.

**Estimated effort**: 1–2 days for diagnosis; fix scope unknown until root cause confirmed  
**Risk**: May require deep CSVR+SETTLE coupling redesign; escalates to full Phase 6 if so.
