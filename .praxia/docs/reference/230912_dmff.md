## Citation
Xinyan Wang, Jichen Li, Lan Yang, Feiyang Chen, Yingze Wang, Junhan Chang, Junmin Chen, Wei Feng, Linfeng Zhang, and Kuang Yu. "DMFF: An Open-Source Automatic Differentiable Platform for Molecular Force Field Development and Molecular Dynamics Simulation." *Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation* 19, no. 17 (2023): 5897–5909. DOI: 10.1021/acs.jctc.2c01297. PubMed: 37589304. Published online: September 12, 2023.

## What it claims
- Fully differentiable implementation of classical and polarizable MM FF models in JAX, enabling gradient-based optimization of FF parameters via autodiff.
- All energy terms — and therefore forces and virial tensors — are computed through JAX's autodiff, enabling both top-down (fit to experiment) and bottom-up (fit to QC) optimization workflows.
- JAX XLA/JIT compilation provides GPU support; "much more efficiently compared to typical Python programs."
- The paper **explicitly acknowledges** DMFF is not as fast as highly optimized C++/CUDA implementations in LAMMPS or OpenMM. For production classical-FF simulations, they recommend rendering DMFF parameters to an OpenMM XML file and running there.
- DMFF's primary value proposition is differentiability, not throughput.
- The frontend API resembles OpenMM, easing adoption for existing OpenMM users.

## Benchmark methodology
- **Hardware tested:** GPU-accelerated (JAX/XLA); specific GPU model not prominently reported in the paper
- **N tested:** Water boxes and small organic molecule systems; exact atom counts not reported. No batched N-molecule benchmark is reported
- **Metric reported:** Gradient correctness and workflow capability, not raw throughput; no head-to-head ns/day table vs. OpenMM
- **Baseline compared against:** OpenMM (qualitative only — DMFF is acknowledged slower for production without gradients)
- **Key acknowledgment:** Authors recommend exporting to OpenMM for non-differentiable production runs — this is itself the performance concession

## Force field scope
- **Bonded terms:** Yes — harmonic bonds, angles, CHARMM-style dihedrals, OPLS-style dihedrals
- **Nonbonded terms:** Yes — full nonbonded: conventional point charge (OPLS/AMBER-like) with PME electrostatics for periodic systems; also multipolar polarizable models (AMOEBA-like, MPID-like)
- **Differentiable?** Yes — this is the core design goal; energies, forces, virial tensors, and thermodynamic quantities are all differentiable

## Relevance to prolix §7.1
- DMFF is JAX-based like prolix — the 10,000× speed gap is not a language-level artifact but a fundamental difference in design intent: differentiable single-system parameter optimization vs. batched N-molecule throughput.
- DMFF includes full nonbonded terms (PME electrostatics, LJ); prolix Scope A is bonded-only. The comparison is conservative for DMFF — it does substantially more work per step.
- DMFF has no concept of batching across N=512 independent molecules; it simulates one system at a time.
- DMFF's own authors acknowledge the throughput gap. §7.1 should frame as: "DMFF trades throughput for differentiability; prolix provides throughput for the parameter-fitting substrate."
- DMFF CPU (14,452 µs) being faster than DMFF A100 (37,850 µs) at N=512 in our benchmark is consistent with single-system JAX: at small N, GPU dispatch overhead dominates when there's no batching.

## Notes
- ChemRxiv preprint: November 2022; JCTC publication: September 12, 2023.
- Maintained by the DeepModeling community (Linfeng Zhang group, DP Technology). Actively developed.
- Supports AMOEBA and MPID polarizable models — genuine capability differentiator beyond AMBER/CHARMM scope; this is a feature gap, not a performance gap.
- The recommended production workflow (render to OpenMM XML) means DMFF's production throughput figures are effectively OpenMM's, not DMFF's own.
- No bonded-only benchmark exists in the paper; all benchmarks appear to include nonbonded terms.
- DMFF GitHub: https://github.com/deepmodeling/DMFF
