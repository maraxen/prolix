## Risk Categories

### 1. **THEORY RISK: LOW**

**Assessment:** The mathematical formulation is grounded in published literature with no documented failure modes.

**Evidence:**
- Zhang et al. (2019): "A unified efficient thermostat scheme for the canonical ensemble with holonomic or isokinetic constraints" (160+ citations, peer-reviewed)
- Peters & Goga (2014): "Stochastic dynamics with correct sampling for constrained systems" (established projection methodology)
- Hartmann & Schütte (2005): Differential geometry foundations for Jacobian-based projections
- Asthagiri & Beck (2023): Empirical validation that feedback loop exists and manifests at dt > 0.5 fs

**Known Unknowns:**
- No published quantitative analysis of timestep stability bounds (why 0.5 fs limit, when 1.0 fs will work)
- No published comparison of Jacobian-projection vs. alternative decoupling methods in literature

**Mitigation:**
- Conduct linear stability analysis during Phase 2 design to derive timestep bounds
- Test against Asthagiri & Beck (2023) benchmark (rigid water equipartition at extended dt)
- Phase 1B: Numerical verification of noise covariance = kT * M * P_rigid via Monte Carlo sampling

**Risk Impact if Realized:** LOW. Derivation error would be caught in Phase 2 implementation (Cholesky failure of G, inconsistent temperature results). Easy to identify and backtrack.

### 2. **IMPLEMENTATION RISK: MEDIUM**

**Assessment:** First JAX implementation of constraint-aware thermostat; numerical stability concerns for degenerate water geometries.

**Specific Risks:**

#### 2.1 Gramian Singularity / Cholesky Failure
- **Scenario**: Water atoms become nearly collinear → G becomes rank-deficient → Cholesky fails
- **Likelihood**: LOW (water angle is ~104°, collinearity requires extreme geometry drift)
- **Impact**: HIGH (simulation crashes if unhandled)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Add regularization: `G_reg = G + (1e-12 * trace(G) / 6.0 + 1.0) * I_6` (implemented in Phase 1 theory pseudocode)
  - Pre-flight check: Verify G eigenvalue ratio < 10^6 before Cholesky
  - Fall back: If G_reg Cholesky fails, switch to standard (full 9D) OU for that water and log warning

#### 2.2 Noise Covariance Accuracy
- **Scenario**: Numerical errors in Cholesky/solve accumulate → noise covariance drifts from kT * M * P_rigid
- **Likelihood**: MEDIUM (7-step numerical pipeline: Jacobian → Gramian → Cholesky → solve → matrix multiply)
- **Impact**: MEDIUM (temperature slightly off-target, may not meet ±5 K tolerance)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Phase 2B: Implement Monte Carlo validation test: draw 10,000 noise samples, verify cov(p_noise) ≈ kT * M * P_rigid numerically
  - Phase 2B: Use double-precision (float64) for G computation, convert back to float32 for momentum update
  - Phase 2B: Benchmark equipartition violation (KS test) on 6D histogram vs. expected distribution

#### 2.3 JAX Differentiability / Autodiff Compatibility
- **Scenario**: Cholesky or lax.scan operations in O-step break gradient computation
- **Likelihood**: LOW (Cholesky is JAX-native, scan is designed for this)
- **Impact**: MEDIUM (required for future parameter optimization, but not blocking for v0.3.0 release)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Phase 2A: Test autodiff with `jax.grad()` on a mock energy function
  - Phase 2A: If issues arise, isolate differentiable path (e.g., precompute G outside gradient)

#### 2.4 Vmap/Scan Efficiency Over N_w Waters
- **Scenario**: Lax.scan over N_w waters becomes bottleneck at large scale (N_w > 1000)
- **Likelihood**: MEDIUM (depends on Cholesky cost; for N_w=1000, 1000 6×6 Choleskys could be slow)
- **Impact**: LOW (v0.3.0 targets small systems; v1.0 prototype scale; not blocking release)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Phase 2: Benchmark scan vs vmap on representative water counts (10, 100, 1000 waters)
  - Phase 5: If scan is bottleneck, consider batched Cholesky (JAX/LAPACK) or GPU optimization in v0.4.0

**Risk Impact if Realized:** MEDIUM. Implementable workarounds exist for all scenarios. Worst-case: fall back to full OU (Langevin without constraints) and keep v1.0's dt ≤ 0.5 fs as primary approach.

### 3. **VALIDATION RISK: MEDIUM-HIGH**

**Assessment:** Key gate is whether constraint-aware Langevin actually stabilizes temperature at dt ≥ 1.0 fs. Theory predicts yes; empirical verification required.

**Critical Validation Gate: Phase 5.1**

- **Test Configuration**: 2-4 TIP3P water molecules, SETTLE + constraint-aware Langevin, dt = 1.0 fs, 100+ ps simulation
- **Gate Criteria**: Mean temperature = 300 ± 5 K (same as v1.0)
- **Expected Outcome**: T stable (no runaway heating) and within tolerance
- **Failure Mode**: T diverges or exceeds ±5 K → indicates constraint-aware approach didn't solve feedback loop
- **If Fails**: Escalate for redesign (Phase 6 Insurance Plan)

**Secondary Validation: Energy Conservation**
- **Test**: Total energy should not drift > 1% over 100 ps
- **Mitigation**: If energy drift > 1%, indicates Cholesky/numerical errors; switch to double-precision for G computation

**Tertiary Validation: Equipartition (Chi-Square Test)**
- **Test**: Compute velocity histogram for 6 rigid DOF per water; verify distribution matches Maxwell-Boltzmann at T = 300 K
- **Method**: Kolmogorov-Smirnov test on 1D projections of velocity distribution
- **Threshold**: KS p-value > 0.05 (fail if < 0.05)
- **If Fails**: Indicates noise covariance drifted from kT * M * P_rigid; revisit Gramian regularization

**Risk Impact if Realized:** HIGH. Validation failure at Phase 5.1 would require major redesign or fallback to v1.0's approach.

## Insurance Plan (If Phase 5.1 Validation Fails)

If constraint-aware Langevin does NOT achieve stable T at dt ≥ 1.0 fs:

**Option A (Recommended):** Accept v1.0 long-term, defer v0.3.0
- Keep v1.0 with dt ≤ 0.5 fs (proven, stable, released)
- Shift v0.3.0 research to v0.4.0 timeline (post-v1.1)
- Investigate alternative approaches: implicit Langevin, higher-order integrators, or alternative constraint algorithms

**Option B:** Pivot to alternative constraint algorithm
- Evaluate LINCS or CCMA as constraint solver instead of SETTLE
- Implement LINCS + Langevin (already studied in literature: Hess et al. 1997)
- Similar timeline (2-3 weeks) but orthogonal implementation
- Risk: LINCS still has constraint-thermostat coupling (per Thallmair et al. 2021), may not solve root cause

**Option C:** Deep-dive into Phase 1 theory refinement
- If validation barely fails (e.g., T = 304 K instead of ≤305), root cause may be Gramian regularization or projection accuracy
- Spend 3-5 days on numerical improvements (double-precision G, better Cholesky, analytical G inversion)
- Re-validate with refined implementation
